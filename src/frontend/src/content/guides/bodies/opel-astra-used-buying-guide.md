## What to check on a used Astra K

The Astra K (2015–2021), sold as Vauxhall in the UK, is lighter and more efficient than the model it replaced. It is an affordable used buy with a few points worth checking.

- **Turbo petrol timing chain.** Some small turbo petrol engines use a timing chain. Listen for a rattle on cold start and ask whether any chain or tensioner work has been carried out. Ask about oil consumption too.
- **Diesel particulate filter.** On CDTi diesels used mainly for short trips, the DPF can clog. Ask whether the car did regular longer runs and whether any emissions warnings have appeared.
- **Cooling system.** Check the temperature gauge behaves normally and ask about any coolant or overheating repairs.
- **Electrics and infotainment.** Test the touchscreen, the IntelliLink system, sensors and electric windows; intermittent electrical faults are worth catching before purchase.
- **Service history.** As a value-focused car, some examples are under-maintained — a continuous service record is reassuring.

## Questions to ask the seller

- Do you have the full service history with stamps or invoices?
- Any timing chain rattle or chain/tensioner work on the petrol engine?
- Has the diesel had DPF or emissions warnings or repairs?
- Does the engine use oil between services?
- Is the registration in your name, and are there two keys?

## Inspection checklist

- Cold-start the engine and listen for chain rattle or other noises.
- Watch the temperature gauge and check the heating/air conditioning works.
- Test the infotainment and electrical features.
- Check tyres for even wear and look for accident-repair signs.
- Verify the VIN against the documents and run an independent history check.

These are general model points, not a verdict on any specific car. Always confirm with documents and a professional inspection before buying.
