## What to check on a used Golf Mk7

The Mk7 (2012–2020) is built on VW's MQB platform and shares engines and gearboxes with the Audi A3, Škoda Octavia and SEAT Leon, so most of the points below apply across that family.

- **DSG gearbox service.** Many Golfs use a DSG automatic. These are reliable when serviced, but the oil/filter change is often skipped. Ask for proof it was done (interval is typically around 60,000 km on the wet clutch). On a test drive, low-speed hesitation, jerking or shuddering is worth investigating further.
- **TSI oil consumption.** Some 1.8/2.0 TSI petrol engines from the earlier part of this generation can use more oil than expected. Ask the owner how often they top up, and check the oil level and condition yourself.
- **Diesel emissions kit.** On 2.0 TDI cars driven mainly on short trips, the DPF (particulate filter) and EGR valve can clog. Ask whether the car regularly does longer runs and whether any emissions warnings have appeared.
- **Timing belt (where fitted).** Several engines in this range use a timing belt with a replacement interval. Confirm whether it has been changed, and when.
- **Infotainment and electrics.** Check the touchscreen, parking sensors, and electric windows all work — small faults here are common and can be fiddly to fix.

## Questions to ask the seller

- Do you have the full service history with stamps or invoices, including any DSG oil change?
- Has the car had any DPF, EGR or emissions-related work or warning lights (diesel)?
- Is there any oil top-up between services, and how much?
- Has the timing belt been replaced, and at what mileage?
- Are there two keys, and is the registration document in your name?

## Inspection checklist

- Cold-start the engine yourself and listen for rattles before it warms up.
- Test the gearbox at low speed and during gentle and firmer acceleration.
- Check tyre wear is even across all four — uneven wear can point to suspension or alignment issues.
- Look for mismatched paint or panel gaps that might indicate past accident repair.
- Confirm the VIN on the car matches the documents, and run an independent history check.

These are general points for the model, not a verdict on any specific car. Always confirm with documents and a professional pre-purchase inspection before buying.
