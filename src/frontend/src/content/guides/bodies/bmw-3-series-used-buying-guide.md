## What to check on a used 3 Series F30

The F30 saloon and F31 Touring (2012–2019) are rear-wheel drive (or xDrive all-wheel drive) and use a range of petrol and diesel engines. They reward careful buying and proper maintenance.

- **Diesel timing chain.** On earlier N47 diesels, the timing chain can wear, and because it sits at the back of the engine, repairs are expensive. Listen for a rattle on cold start and ask whether any chain work has been done. Later B47 engines improved this.
- **Cooling system.** The electric water pump and thermostat are wear items on these engines. Ask about any overheating or cooling repairs and check the temperature behaves normally on the drive.
- **Oil leaks.** With age, gaskets such as the valve cover and oil filter housing can seep. Look under the car and around the engine for oil residue.
- **Run-flat tyres and no spare.** Many came on run-flats with no spare wheel. Check tyre condition, that all four match, and budget for their higher replacement cost.
- **Gearbox and electronics.** Most are automatic; confirm smooth shifts and that iDrive, sensors and electronics work. Ask about any service of the automatic gearbox.

## Questions to ask the seller

- Full service history — main dealer or BMW specialist?
- Any timing chain work or cold-start rattle on the diesel?
- Has the cooling system (water pump/thermostat) been replaced?
- Are the run-flat tyres a matched set, and is there a spare or repair kit?
- Is the registration in your name, and are there two keys?

## Inspection checklist

- Cold-start the engine and listen carefully for rattles, especially on diesels.
- Watch the temperature gauge and feel for smooth gearbox shifts on the drive.
- Inspect under the engine and car for oil leaks.
- Check tyre wear and brand match, and look for accident-repair signs.
- Verify the VIN against the documents and run an independent history check.

These are general model points, not a verdict on any specific car. Always confirm with documents and a professional inspection before buying.
