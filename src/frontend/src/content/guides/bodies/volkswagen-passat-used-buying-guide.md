## What to check on a used Passat B8

The B8 (2014–2023) shares its MQB platform, engines and gearboxes with the Golf, Škoda Superb and Audi A4-class cars. Because so many were fleet vehicles, focus on how the car was used and maintained.

- **Service history depth.** High mileage is normal for a Passat and not a problem in itself — but only if servicing was kept up. Look for a continuous record with stamps or invoices rather than a single recent service.
- **DSG gearbox.** Most have a DSG automatic. Confirm the oil/filter service was done, and feel for smooth shifts at low speed and under acceleration on the test drive.
- **Diesel emissions system.** On the popular 2.0 TDI, the DPF and EGR can clog on cars used only for short trips. On later cars, AdBlue dosing faults can trigger warnings. Ask about any emissions lights or related repairs.
- **Adaptive suspension (DCC).** If fitted, check the ride modes function and listen for knocks over bumps; replacement dampers are not cheap.
- **Electronics.** Test the infotainment, the digital cockpit (where fitted), parking sensors and the tailgate; intermittent electrical niggles are worth catching before purchase.

## Questions to ask the seller

- Was the car a private, company or fleet vehicle, and do you have its full history?
- Has the DSG had its scheduled oil and filter change?
- Any DPF, EGR or AdBlue warnings or repairs on the diesel?
- Does it mostly do motorway miles or short local trips?
- Is the V5/registration in your name, and are there two keys?

## Inspection checklist

- Start the engine from cold and check for smoke or rattles.
- Drive at varied speeds to assess the gearbox and any suspension knocks.
- Inspect tyres for even wear and check the spare/repair kit is present.
- Look for repaint or uneven panel gaps suggesting accident history.
- Match the VIN to the documents and run an independent vehicle history check.

These are general points for the model, not a judgement on any specific car. Confirm everything with documents and a professional inspection before you buy.
