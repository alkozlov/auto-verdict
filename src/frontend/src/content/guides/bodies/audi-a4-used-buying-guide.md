## What to check on a used A4 B9

The B9 (2015–2023) shares much of its engineering with the VW Passat and Audi A5. It is a polished car, but as a premium model the cost of neglected maintenance or electronic faults can be high.

- **Gearbox service.** Many B9s use the S tronic dual-clutch automatic. Confirm its oil/clutch service was done on schedule and check for smooth, hesitation-free shifts at low speed.
- **TFSI oil consumption.** Ask whether the petrol engine uses oil between services and check the level and condition. Treat a vague answer as a prompt for closer inspection.
- **Diesel emissions system.** On TDI cars, ask about DPF, EGR and AdBlue behaviour, and whether the car did regular longer journeys rather than only short trips.
- **Electronics and Virtual Cockpit.** Test the MMI infotainment, digital instrument display, cameras, sensors and electric tailgate. Premium electronics are expensive to repair out of warranty.
- **Adaptive suspension and quattro.** If fitted, check the ride modes work and listen for knocks. On quattro cars, confirm any driveline servicing was carried out.

## Questions to ask the seller

- Do you have the full Audi or specialist service history?
- Has the S tronic gearbox had its scheduled oil/clutch service?
- Does the engine use oil between services?
- Any diesel emissions (DPF/EGR/AdBlue) warnings or repairs?
- Is the car still in warranty, and is the registration in your name?

## Inspection checklist

- Cold-start the engine and check for smoke, rattles or warning lights.
- Drive at varied speeds to test the gearbox and suspension.
- Work through every electronic feature methodically.
- Check tyre wear is even and look for accident-repair signs (repaint, panel gaps).
- Match the VIN to the documents and run an independent history check.

These are general model points, not an assessment of any specific car. Confirm everything with documents and a professional pre-purchase inspection before buying.
