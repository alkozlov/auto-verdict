## What to check on a used Octavia Mk3

The Mk3 (2013–2020) sits on the same MQB platform as the VW Golf and uses the same engines and DSG gearboxes, so the mechanical checks are identical. Because many Octavias served as taxis or fleet cars, pay particular attention to how this one was used.

- **Previous use and mileage.** Establish whether the car was private, fleet or taxi. High mileage is fine if servicing kept pace; the risk is heavy use with skipped maintenance.
- **DSG gearbox service.** If it has a DSG automatic, confirm the oil and filter change was done and check for smooth low-speed shifting on the test drive.
- **TSI oil use.** Some petrol engines in this range can consume oil; ask about top-ups and check the level and condition.
- **Diesel DPF/EGR.** On TDI cars used for short trips, the particulate filter and EGR can clog. Ask about emissions warnings and whether the car did regular longer runs.
- **Interior wear vs stated mileage.** On a high-mileage workhorse, check the driver's seat bolster, steering wheel and pedals match the odometer reading.

## Questions to ask the seller

- Was this car privately owned, or used as a fleet or taxi vehicle?
- Do you have the complete service history, including any DSG oil change?
- Any diesel emissions warnings or DPF/EGR repairs?
- Does the petrol engine use oil between services?
- Is the registration in your name, and are there two keys?

## Inspection checklist

- Cold-start the car and listen before it warms up.
- Test the gearbox at low and higher speeds.
- Check tyres for even wear and confirm the spare/repair kit is present.
- Look for repaint, overspray or uneven panel gaps from accident repair.
- Verify the VIN against the documents and run an independent history check.

These are general model points, not a verdict on any specific car. Always confirm with documents and a professional inspection before buying.
