## What to check on a used Focus

The Mk3 (2011–2018) and Mk4 (2018 onwards) Focus are good to drive and widely available used. Two drivetrain points deserve particular attention.

- **Powershift automatic (Mk3).** Many Mk3 cars used a dry dual-clutch "Powershift" automatic that became known for shuddering, hesitation and clutch wear. On the test drive, feel for shudder when pulling away and ask for any gearbox repair or clutch-replacement history. A manual avoids the issue.
- **EcoBoost wet timing belt.** Several 1.0 EcoBoost engines use a timing belt that runs in oil and must be replaced on schedule. Ask whether it has been done and when, as neglect can lead to expensive damage. On early units, also ask about any coolant or overheating history.
- **Service history.** Look for a continuous record. Budget cars are sometimes under-maintained, so documentation is reassuring.
- **Suspension and steering.** Listen for knocks over bumps and check for uneven tyre wear that can hint at worn suspension or alignment.
- **Electronics.** Test the infotainment (SYNC), windows, air conditioning and sensors.

## Questions to ask the seller

- Is it a manual or Powershift automatic, and does the auto have any gearbox/clutch repair history?
- Has the EcoBoost wet timing belt been replaced, and at what mileage?
- Any coolant, overheating or head-related repairs?
- Do you have the full service history?
- Is the registration in your name, and are there two keys?

## Inspection checklist

- Pull away gently and firmly to feel for any gearbox shudder (automatic).
- Cold-start the engine and check for smoke, rattles or warning lights.
- Drive over rough surfaces to listen for suspension knocks.
- Check tyres for even wear and look for accident-repair signs.
- Verify the VIN against the documents and run an independent history check.

These are general model points, not a verdict on any specific car. Always confirm with documents and a professional inspection before buying.
