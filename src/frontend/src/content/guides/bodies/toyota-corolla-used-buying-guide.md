## What to check on a used Corolla

The current Corolla (E210, 2018 onwards) is sold as petrol and self-charging hybrid. Reliability is a strong point, so checks focus on confirming that good reputation holds for the specific car.

- **Hybrid battery health.** Ask whether the car had its regular hybrid health checks at Toyota — in many markets these extend the hybrid battery warranty. Find out if any warranty still applies and whether the battery has ever been serviced or replaced.
- **Brake condition.** Regenerative braking means the friction brakes do less work and can corrode or seize from light use, particularly in wet climates. Have them inspected; listen for grinding or feel for uneven braking.
- **Service history.** A documented Toyota or specialist history is reassuring and can protect warranty cover. Look for continuity rather than a single recent stamp.
- **Tyres and underbody.** Check tyre wear is even and inspect the underbody for corrosion or kerb/pothole damage.
- **Software and recalls.** Ask whether any outstanding recalls or software updates have been completed.

## Questions to ask the seller

- Was the car serviced at Toyota or a qualified specialist, with records?
- Have the scheduled hybrid health checks been done, and does any hybrid battery warranty remain?
- When were the brakes last inspected or serviced?
- Are there any outstanding recalls or warning lights?
- Is the registration in your name, and are there two keys?

## Inspection checklist

- Start the car and confirm the hybrid system powers up with no warning lights.
- On the drive, check the transition between electric and petrol is smooth and braking is even.
- Inspect brakes and tyres for wear or corrosion.
- Look for accident-repair signs such as repaint or uneven panel gaps.
- Verify the VIN against the documents and run an independent history check.

These are general model points, not a verdict on any specific car. Always confirm with documents and a professional inspection before buying.
